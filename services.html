Process

What working with me is meant to feel like

1

Understanding the Issue

We want to make a clear description of what happened, when it started, and what may have changed to cause the the difference. It may be a simple fix or a complicated issue. Sometimes differences are okay and may not be an issue, but we need to look into what it is.

2

Explain the Options

If there are multiple solutions, I want to make sure you know the benifits and cost of each solution. Sometimes there may be a simple cheap fix, but it you want it to last a long time, we might want to look into some other options.

3

Work on Solutions

The goal is to make sure you leave satisfied with the results. If there is a budget and a goal, we will try our best to make sure you have what you need at the price you can afford and test it all to make sure it follows our expectation guidelines.

4

Leave Things Clearer

Good support should leave you more informed than when we started. We are not here just to fix something and leave. We want you to understand what was done, why it mattered, and how it helped your system. Our goal is to make technology feel less confusing, so you feel more confident with your own devices and know what to look for if a similar issue comes up again.

Q & A

Common questions

Are these services meant to show current skills or future business direction?

Both. This page represents what I can already speak to and help with, while also showing the direction I want MR Techworks to keep growing toward.

Do you only focus on advanced technical work?

No. Some of the most helpful work is basic support done clearly and patiently, especially when someone just needs the problem explained in a useful way. Most IT work is simple and is bet used with great customer service. Getting to know people and understanding there needs can mean more than the actual work itself.

Why does my Wi-Fi work in one room but not another?

Wi-Fi can be affected by distance, walls, floors, appliances, old equipment, and how your router is placed. Sometimes the problem is not your internet provider, but the way the signal is moving through your home. I want to help you understand where the problem is actually coming from before you spend money on upgrades you may not need.

Do I need a new computer, or can mine still be fixed?

Not every slow computer needs to be replaced. Sometimes a cleanup, storage upgrade, software fix, or better understanding of what is running in the background can make a big difference. My goal is to help you figure out whether your device still has life left in it or if replacing it would genuinely be the better choice.

Why is my computer so slow?

A slow computer can be caused by low storage, too many startup programs, outdated hardware, background apps, malware, or simply age. The important part is not guessing. I want to look at what is actually causing the slowdown and explain it in a way that makes sense, so you know whether it is a small fix, a bigger issue, or a sign that it may be time to upgrade.

How do I know if something is a scam?

Scams often create pressure. They may say your account is locked, your device is infected, or you need to act immediately. Real companies usually do not demand gift cards, remote access, or urgent payment through strange links. I want to help people slow down, check the warning signs, and feel more confident before clicking, replying, or paying for something suspicious.

Is it safe to let someone remote into my computer?

Remote support can be safe when you know who you are working with and you gave permission first. It becomes dangerous when a stranger calls, messages, or pops up on your screen claiming they need access right away. I want to help you understand when remote support makes sense and when it is a red flag.

What should I do before buying a new laptop or computer?

Before buying, you should think about what you actually need it for: school, work, gaming, video calls, storage, travel, or basic browsing. A more expensive computer is not always the right computer. I want to help you choose something that fits your needs and budget instead of pushing you toward features you may never use.

Why does my device keep running out of storage?

Storage fills up from photos, videos, downloads, old files, apps, backups, and sometimes hidden system data. Deleting random things can help, but it can also get confusing fast. I want to help you clean things up safely and understand what is taking up space, so you do not accidentally delete something important.

Should I repair my device or replace it?

That depends on the age of the device, the cost of the repair, how well it performs, and what you need it to do. Sometimes a repair makes sense. Other times, putting money into an old device may not be worth it. I want to give honest advice, even if that means telling you not to spend money on a repair that will not help much long term.

Why does my printer never work when I need it?

Printers are weird little drama machines. Common issues include Wi-Fi problems, driver issues, paper jams, low ink, wrong default printer settings, or the printer being connected to a different network. I want to help make the problem less frustrating by checking the simple stuff first and explaining what went wrong.

Can you teach me how to fix small problems myself?

Yes. I do not want you to feel helpless with your own technology. Some problems should be handled carefully, but there are plenty of small things you can learn to check safely. I want to explain what I am doing, why I am doing it, and what signs to look for next time, so you feel more confident using your devices.

Why does my phone, computer, or tablet keep asking for updates?

Updates often fix bugs, improve security, and help apps work correctly. They can be annoying, but ignoring them for too long can cause problems later. I want to help you understand which updates matter, when to install them, and how to avoid update issues when possible.

What information should I back up?

Important files, school or work documents, photos, videos, passwords, and anything you would be upset to lose should be backed up. Devices can be replaced, but personal files are much harder to recover. I want to help people set up simple backup habits before something goes wrong, not after.

Why does my computer say it has a virus?

Sometimes the warning is real. Sometimes it is a fake pop-up trying to scare you. The safest thing to do is avoid clicking the warning until you know where it came from. I want to help you tell the difference between a real security issue and a scare tactic.

Why should I ask for tech help instead of just Googling it?

Searching online can help, but it can also lead to outdated advice, confusing steps, unnecessary purchases, or risky downloads. The internet gives you answers, but not always the right answer for your situation. I want to help sort through the noise and give you guidance that actually fits your device, your problem, and your comfort level.

What makes your tech help different?

I want my services to feel clear, honest, and useful. I am not just trying to fix something and leave. I want you to understand what happened, what was done, and what it means for your device or setup. Good tech help should leave you with a working solution and a little more confidence than you had before.
