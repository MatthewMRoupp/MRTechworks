Services

Practical tech support with a patient, people-first approach.

This section outlines the kinds of help I can provide now and continue growing into over time. The goal is simple: make technology feel more manageable, more understandable, and more useful.

How I can help

Support areas that fit the direction of this website

01

General troubleshooting

Help identifying what is going wrong with a device, application, or setup and turning that into clear steps for fixing it.

  • Slow devices or confusing behavior
  • Basic software setup and organization
  • Researching likely causes before replacing anything

02

Home tech and network guidance

Support for Wi-Fi layout, device connectivity, and making a home setup easier to understand and manage.

  • Wi-Fi and mesh placement ideas
  • Connection troubleshooting
  • Basic network organization and device separation

03

Buying advice and hardware planning

Thoughtful recommendations for parts, upgrades, and technology purchases based on budget and goals.

  • PC part comparison and compatibility checks
  • Upgrade path suggestions
  • Explaining tradeoffs without the jargon overload

04

Simple web and digital help

Guidance for clean portfolio-style sites, content layout decisions, and presenting technical work more clearly online.

  • Portfolio website structure
  • Content organization and page planning
  • Improving clarity for class or career projects

Process

What working with me is meant to feel like

01

Understand the issue

I want the real context first, not just the symptom.

02

Explain the options

If there are multiple paths, I focus on the tradeoffs in plain language.

03

Work toward the fix

The goal is useful progress, not technical theater.

04

Leave things clearer

Good support should leave the person more informed than when they started.

Good fit for

  • A person frustrated by a setup that is not working right

  • Anyone who values communication as much as the final answer
